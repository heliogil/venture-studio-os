# Experiment Criteria

Not every idea earns a factory sprint. These are the criteria a venture must meet before we write the first packet.

## The filter

A venture gets a Sprint 0 if it passes all four:

### 1. The market exists and is reachable

We need evidence — not a hypothesis — that people are spending money on this problem today. Sources: Reddit threads with pain, job posts, competitor revenue estimates, failed alternatives.

**Disqualifier:** "People will pay for this" based on intuition alone.

### 2. The moat is a data asset or distribution, not the product

Products get copied. Data pipelines, normalised price histories, proprietary audiences — these compound. The venture must have a clear answer to "what do we own after 12 months that competitors can't replicate in a weekend?"

**Disqualifier:** The only moat is "we built it first."

### 3. The founder's time constraint is met

Operations cost ≤ 10h/week post-launch (≤ 15–20h launch week). If the business requires the founder to be a full-time operator, it's a job, not a venture.

**Disqualifier:** Requires constant manual intervention to function.

### 4. The factory can build the v1

The core product can be built by Ergon/Argus without capabilities we don't have today. External dependencies (payment processors, data providers, regulatory approvals) are mapped and either resolved or time-boxed.

**Disqualifier:** Requires a human senior developer full-time.

## Sprint 0 deliverable

A Sprint 0 produces a **CONDITIONAL GO / NO-GO** decision with:
- Competitive landscape (3 competitors, their weaknesses)
- Moat hypothesis (what we'll own that compounds)
- Technical feasibility (what the factory can and can't do)
- Kill criteria (written before Sprint 1 starts)
- Gate metrics (what 90 days post-launch must show)
